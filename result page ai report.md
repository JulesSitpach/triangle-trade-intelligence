The Problem: Your smartphone assembly qualifies for USMCA at exactly the 65% North American content threshold with no buffer. This sourcing decision was likely driven by cost optimization and technical specifications, but it creates a structural vulnerability. Your Mexico and Canada sourcing (65% combined) provides the USMCA qualification floor, but leaves zero margin for supply chain disruptions, quality failures, or regulatory changes that could disqualify components from preference treatment.

Sarah Chen should act now because the current tariff environment remains stable, allowing time for planned nearshoring rather than reactive supply chain restructuring.
